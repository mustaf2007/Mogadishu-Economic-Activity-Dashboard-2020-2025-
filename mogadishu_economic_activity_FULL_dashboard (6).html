Executive Summary

This interactive dashboard analyzes spatial and temporal patterns of economic activity in Mogadishu between 2020 and 2025 using satellite-derived night-time lights as a proxy.

Key Takeaways

  • Economic activity increased overall across Mogadishu.
  • The eastern zone consistently exhibits higher economic intensity.
  • All zones experienced recovery after 2020.
  • Spatial concentration increased over time.

Data Sources & Credits

Satellite Data:
VIIRS Day/Night Band (DNB) monthly night-time lights, processed using Google Earth Engine.

Spatial Framework:
Custom grid-based urban analysis over Mogadishu, used in place of administrative districts due to boundary limitations.

Methodology:
Night-time light intensity is used as a proxy for economic activity, capturing both formal and informal urban dynamics.

Disclaimer:
Night-time lights are an indirect proxy and do not measure GDP, income, or welfare directly. Results indicate relative spatial and temporal patterns of economic activity.
